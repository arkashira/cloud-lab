# Customer Journey Map: cloud-lab

## 1. **Aware**

**Trigger Event:** Searches for "free AWS practice account" or "AWS sandbox" on Google.

**Friction Points:**
- Limited visibility on cloud-lab's website due to lack of SEO optimization.
- Insufficient social proof (e.g., testimonials, reviews) to build credibility.

**User Emotions:**
- Curiosity
- Skepticism

**Opportunities to Delight:**
- Improve SEO to increase visibility.
- Showcase success stories and testimonials from early users.
- Offer a free trial with no credit card required.

**Metric:** Increase in organic traffic by 20% within the first 3 months.

## 2. **Consider**

**Trigger Event:** Lands on cloud-lab's website and explores features.

**Friction Points:**
- Complex pricing structure (if any) that's not immediately clear.
- Lack of comparison with other free alternatives (e.g., AWS Educate, A Cloud Guru).

**User Emotions:**
- Confusion
- Comparison shopping

**Opportunities to Delight:**
- Simplify pricing structure and highlight it prominently.
- Create a comparison page showcasing cloud-lab's advantages over other free alternatives.
- Offer a webinar or demo to showcase cloud-lab's unique features.

**Metric:** Decrease in bounce rate by 15% and increase in time spent on the website by 25%.

## 3. **Try**

**Trigger Event:** Signs up for a free account using email or GitHub.

**Friction Points:**
- Lengthy sign-up process with excessive form fields.
- Slow account activation process.

**User Emotions:**
- Frustration
- Anticipation

**Opportunities to Delight:**
- Streamline sign-up process and reduce form fields.
- Implement instant account activation upon sign-up.
- Send a welcome email with quick-start guide and tips.

**Metric:** Increase in sign-up conversion rate by 15% and decrease in support tickets related to account activation by 30%.

## 4. **Adopt**

**Trigger Event:** Successfully creates and interacts with AWS services in the cloud-lab sandbox.

**Friction Points:**
- Limited service offerings compared to a full AWS account.
- Occasional service disruptions or slow performance.

**User Emotions:**
- Satisfaction
- Frustration (due to service limitations or disruptions)

**Opportunities to Delight:**
- Regularly add new services based on user demand.
- Implement a robust monitoring system to minimize service disruptions.
- Send personalized emails highlighting new features or services.

**Metric:** Increase in active users (defined as users who log in and interact with services) by 20% month-over-month.

## 5. **Expand**

**Trigger Event:** User becomes proficient in AWS services and considers using cloud-lab for team collaboration or production-like environments.

**Friction Points:**
- Lack of collaboration features (e.g., team accounts, shared projects).
- Inadequate security and compliance features for production use cases.

**User Emotions:**
- Excitement
- Frustration (due to lack of advanced features)

**Opportunities to Delight:**
- Introduce team accounts and collaboration features.
- Enhance security and compliance features to support production environments.
- Offer priority support and customization options for enterprise users.

**Metric:** Increase in team accounts by 15% quarter-over-quarter and increase in enterprise users by 10% quarter-over-quarter.

**Revenue Metric:** Achieve $500,000 in annual recurring revenue (ARR) from enterprise users within the first year of launching the expansion phase.
